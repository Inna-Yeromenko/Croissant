Ah the croissant, the iconic French pastry... What if we were to tell you that history of the croissant suggests that it may not be French at all!? The origin of the croissant can actually be traced back to 13th century Austria, where it was called the kipferl, the German word for crescent. Croissants are part of the category of pastries that the French call Viennoiseries, a nod to their origins in Vienna.
